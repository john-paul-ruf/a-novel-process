### 3. The Shuttle Sequence — Real Temptation, Not Neat Capitulation
✅ **COMPLETE**

**Core mechanism locked**: The colony stays not out of faith in the colony, but out of **distrust of the corporation**. When Cole asks "How many other charters have invoked 9.4.2?", the representative deflects into corporate speak: "Questions regarding competing charter structures are best addressed through our formal arbitration process—which, as you're aware, requires functional communications infrastructure." The response confirms the arbitration is rigged (they control it) without saying it directly. The evasion *is* the answer.

**Garrett Polk's temptation**: "Debts forgiven" genuinely seizes him—it's the threshold back to custody, credibility, his daughter. He calculates visibly, weights both sides with engineer's precision. When the colony chooses to stay, Polk chooses against himself. Not ideology. Refusal to watch the trap spring twice.

**Why they stay**: Because the corporation won't answer a simple yes-or-no. Because the systems that were supposed to protect them are all locked in the company's hands. Because staying is harder than leaving, but trusting Asterlyne would be stupider.

## What to Protect

- **The count as pulse**: The running death toll is *not* just accounting—it's Cole's emotional landscape rendered in arithmetic. Every number is a face. Don't soften this. Lean into it.
- **Elena's logs as ghost narrative**: The way they fracture across the novel, read in stolen moments, with growing dread—that structure is airtight. Keep the fragments, keep the non-linear reading order, keep the dread.
- **The ending restraint**: "Tuesday." Water filtration line. Sitting in dirt. The message to Lily. This is perfect. Don't add ceremony. Don't make it bigger than it is.
- **Clause 9.4.2 as the mechanism**: The contract as both trap and key. Corporate language weaponized against its authors. This is too good to dilute. Keep it central.
- **Cole's voice as reticent**: He doesn't give speeches. He does the work. He shows the math. He sits with people at their worst and doesn't look away. That restraint is what makes him trustworthy.
