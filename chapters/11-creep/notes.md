# Chapter 11 — Author Notes

## Voice Decisions

Cole's interiority dominates — the chapter lives in his calculation and observation rather than in dramatic tension or dialogue. The creep is mysterious but not supernatural; it's a biological system, which makes it more unsettling than if it were simply dangerous. Cole's response is pragmatic (research, planning, relocation) rather than reactive (panic, aggression). Polk's confrontation outside the council shelter ("You've got a plan") sharpens the political friction and gives Polk a voice moment that plants his later evolution.

## Structure

The chapter moves from discovery → understanding (via Dana's research) → first death → strategic response (Cole's mapping scene) → contested vote. The death of Marcus Reeves is handled with dignity but without sentimentality — Haruki provides mercy through clinical economy (one sentence, not three), and Cole preserves privacy through presence rather than decree. The colony's reaction to the creep death is differentiated from prior deaths — this one sits differently because it's not human-scaled.

The Turn scene (Cole mapping the advance rate on his shelter floor) was added to show Cole doing the math the outline requires, and to drive home the "Elena never solved this" beat. Cole's confrontation with Elena's competence-that-wasn't-enough is the chapter's emotional spine.

The vote is close (21-17, Ren abstaining) per the scene outline, restoring the intended dramatic tension. Cole resists the vote ("A vote takes time we don't have"), Ren pushes back ("Then what are we surviving for?"), and the colony splits. This feeds directly into Ch 12's relocation under political fracture.
