## Author Notes — Chapter 1

**Voice decisions:**
- Embedded Cole's counting obsession into the prose rhythm itself—short sentences ("Cole read.") breaking up longer ones to mirror how his mind works: methodical processing interrupted by sharp clarity.
- "Performing the social shapes of talking" — inhabiting the character's observational distance; he sees people as systems to evaluate.
- Minimal dialogue (only Polk speaks). Cole's voice is interior; he doesn't perform for others.
- Embedded technical reading (the charter quotes) within interiority—exposition delivered through Cole's mental process, not as summary.
- Emotional gut-punch reserved for the Lily section: longer sentences, more vulnerable rhythm, physical details (the colored pencil horse, the table, the recycled bin) grounding abstraction.

**Structural choices:**
- Opening moves from external observation (room, people) to Cole's singular focus (reading) to his interior calculation—funneling outward → inward → the dark decision.
- Charter reading is structurally the spine: moves from Section 1 → Sections 2-5 → Section 6 → appendices. Each layer reveals more.
- Garrett Polk appears twice: once observed (the man "not reading"), once direct address. His understanding of clause 9.4.2 mirrors Cole's—both read all the way through. This parallel sets up their future ideological collision.
- Final paragraph is Cole's internal understanding of his own choice. Not yet aware it will consume the next five years.

**Passages to flag for client review:**
- "Performing the social shapes of talking" — checking if this hits the right tone of anthropological observation or if it reads as too writerly.
- The Lily section (the horse, the recruitment email). Checking for emotional authenticity—does the casual abandonment land as true, or does it need more justification?
- Garrett Polk's exit line. Checking if his bitterness reads as character-driven or if it needs more grounding in his specific wound.
