## Author Notes — Chapter 5

**Voice decisions:**
- Deep interiority carries all three death sequences. Cole processes each death through the lens of systems failure: Lev's death is a compliance failure, Priya's is a calculation he made, Vogel's is irreducible human error. Each death teaches him something different about the limits of optimization.
- Dialogue kept minimal and functional. The Haruki exchange at the body ("Would it have mattered?" / "Maybe.") does triple duty: reveals the protocol gap, establishes that the planet creates vulnerabilities beyond screening, and shows Haruki's honesty-as-kindness mode.
- Meeting dialogue tightened to outline specification. Ren's "People aren't margin" and Cole's "They are if you want them to be alive" are the only exchange. No extension, no "hope" speech, no premature emotional vulnerability. Cole is still the calculator at this stage — the math doesn't crack until Ch 16.
- Fragment trio ("Simple. Stupid. Human.") used for the third death's thematic punch. Voice profile supports fragments for emphasis. Mirrors the outline's exact phrasing.

**Structural choices:**
- Four sections: three deaths, one reckoning. Section breaks create a rhythmic counting effect — death, death, death, the math. Each section shorter than the last until the meeting scene expands, mirroring the compression of loss and the expansion of consequence.
- Third death deliberately stripped of exotic biology. Vogel dies from gravity and bad footing — the simplest possible cause. This reserves the antibiotic-resistance beat for Ch 8 (Sarah), where it properly functions as the first crack in Cole's faith that optimization guarantees survival.
- Meeting aftermath expanded to deliver the Turn: colony's split perception of Cole. Polk and Joss both present — Polk running his own math that doesn't match Cole's, Joss watching his transit preparation collide with reality. These threads connect to Ch 7 (Polk's faction) and Ch 16 (Joss's fever, Cole's vulnerability).
