# Story Bible — THE FREE COLONY

## Characters

### Primary POV

**Cole Maren**
- Physical description: 38 at signing. Average height, lean build that gets leaner. Hands that look like they've carried things — not rough, just used. Brown hair kept short for practicality. Face that defaults to neutral; people read into it what they want to see (leader, machine, threat).
- Age: 38 (start), 43 (end)
- Speech patterns: Minimal. Says what needs saying and stops. Uses numbers and specifics instead of abstractions. Deflects personal questions. When emotional, gets quieter, not louder. Rare dry humor — observational, never performative.
- Arc: Calculator → reluctant leader → father-in-practice. Starts as a man running arithmetic on his own survival. Ends as a man who builds things for people he loves. The math never stops — but it stops being the reason.
- First appearance: Ch 1

**Ren Tsai**
- Physical description: 34. Compact, athletic. Short black hair, usually pushed back. Engineer's hands — always fiddling with something. Direct eye contact that some people find aggressive.
- Age: 34 (start), 39 (end)
- Speech patterns: Direct, slightly combative. Asks questions that are really arguments. Uses "we" more than Cole does. Speaks faster under stress. Interrupts.
- Arc: Idealist → pragmatic democrat. Starts seeing Cole as the problem (autocracy in survival's clothing). Ends seeing him as the complement — she builds the system, he keeps it breathing. Learns that democracy is harder than principle.
- First appearance: Ch 2

**Dana Wen**
- Physical description: 29. Slim, quiet-bodied — takes up less space than she needs to. Dark hair usually tied back. Hands perpetually soil-stained. Face that people forget because she makes sure they do.
- Age: 29 (start), 34 (end)
- Speech patterns: Soft-spoken, precise. Uses scientific terminology casually. Answers direct questions directly but volunteers nothing. When lying, she doesn't change — that's what makes her good at it.
- Arc: Compliant debtor → conflicted traitor → confessor → earned member. The most morally complex character. She saves the colony with the same hands that betray it. Her arc is about learning that debt doesn't justify destruction, and that worth is demonstrated, not calculated.
- First appearance: Ch 2 (background), Ch 8 (featured), Ch 14 (first POV)

### Secondary Characters

**Haruki Sato**
- Physical description: 52. Tall, slightly stooped. Gray at the temples. Steady hands. Wears the same expression during a crisis as during dinner — measured calm that isn't performance, it's constitution.
- Age: 52 (start), 57 (end)
- Speech patterns: Gentle, precise. Asks "How are you?" and means it diagnostically. Uses silence as a conversational tool. Never raises his voice.
- Arc: Colony doctor, moral compass. Doesn't change dramatically — he's the steady point others orbit. His consistency is what makes his rare moments of fear devastating.
- First appearance: Ch 2

**Joss Carver**
- Physical description: 22. Gangly, hasn't quite grown into his frame. Red-brown hair, freckled. Moves too fast for his body — bumps into things, drops things, catches them. Eager face.
- Age: 22 (start), 27 (end)
- Speech patterns: Talks too much when nervous (which is often). Asks questions. Uses filler words ("like," "I mean"). Over-explains. Matures noticeably — by Year 4, the filler is gone.
- Arc: Anxious kid → colony builder. Becomes the person Cole parents as a surrogate for Lily. Doesn't replace her — fills the space Cole's fatherhood instincts need. By Year 5, he's the one organizing the colony's first celebration.
- First appearance: Ch 2

**Garrett Polk**
- Physical description: 45. Broad, ex-contractor's build. Loud face — expressions carry across a room. Sunburned permanently.
- Age: 45 (start), 50 (end)
- Speech patterns: Loud, declarative. Talks about freedom without defining it. Interrupts. Gets folksy when he wants something. Smarter than he performs.
- Arc: Antagonist → reluctant participant → surprising vote for mercy. Starts as the colony's loudest dissenter. Nearly destroys the charter with a rescue signal. Gets neutralized by Ren's compromise. Surprises everyone by voting to keep Dana — pragmatism over grudge.
- First appearance: Ch 2

**Elena Vasik** *(voice only — expedition logs)*
- Physical description: N/A (described only through writing voice)
- Age: Unknown. Reads as mid-40s from tone.
- Speech patterns (in logs): Clinical scientist at first — data-driven, precise. Gradually cracks. Entries get shorter, more personal, more raw. Final entry is two words: "Enough, now."
- Arc: Runs parallel to Cole's but fails. Same planet, fewer resources, no ownership clause, no clause 9.4.2. Her colony died because they stopped being a group. Her logs are the ghost narrative — warning, map, mirror.
- First appearance: Ch 6 (logs discovered)

**Lily Maren** *(off-screen — present in memory and messages)*
- Physical description: 9 at start. Likes drawing horses. Brown hair like Cole's.
- Arc: The wound that becomes the reason. Never appears on Parcel 16b. Present in Cole's interiority, in his messages, in the spaces between what he says and what he means. She is the unanswered question that drives him: can he become someone worth coming back to?
- First appearance: Ch 1 (memory)

**Suri Park**
- Physical description: Late 20s. First colonist to suffer a serious alien biology incident (fruit toxicity).
- Arc: Survivor of Ch 4 incident. Reminder that the planet's rules change.
- First appearance: Ch 4

## Locations

**Asterlyne Corp Onboarding Center (Earth)**
- Description: Corporate sterile. Waiting room with forty-seven chairs. Fluorescent light. Contract tablets on a table.
- Key details: The last Earth interior Cole sees. Should feel suffocating.
- Scenes set here: Ch 1

**Asterlyne Transport Vessel**
- Description: Company ship built for cost, not comfort. Recycled air. Narrow bunks. A common mess that doubles as orientation room. Three months of confinement.
- Key details: The crucible where factions form. Orientation videos are propaganda.
- Scenes set here: Ch 2

**Parcel 16b — Settlement Zone (Original)**
- Description: Clearing in a forest of spiral-trunked trees. Lavender-gray soil. Sky shifted slightly green. Air smells of ozone and sweet rot. Trees hum below hearing. Pre-fab shelters arranged in a grid — corporate efficiency, not human logic.
- Key details: Abandoned after creep threat in Ch 12. Reclaimed by forest by Year 5. Shuttle lands here in Ch 24.
- Scenes set here: Ch 3-11

**Parcel 16b — Settlement Zone (Relocated)**
- Description: Higher ground, rock substrate. Ancient channels visible in exposed bedrock. More defensible. Built by the colonists, not Asterlyne's pre-fabs — rougher but theirs.
- Key details: The settlement the colony names. Home.
- Scenes set here: Ch 12-27

**Ghost Colony (Previous Expedition Site)**
- Description: 12km west. Collapsed pre-fab shelters overgrown by spiral trees. Equipment rusted and half-absorbed by the planet. Sealed data core containing Elena Vasik's logs.
- Key details: A grave and a warning. Cole builds a cairn here in Ch 23.
- Scenes set here: Ch 6, Ch 23

**The Forest**
- Description: Spiral-trunked trees that hum at sub-audible frequencies. Lavender understory. Fauna that watches but rarely approaches. The creep originates here during second orbital cycle.
- Key details: Beautiful and wrong. The boundary between colony and planet.
- Scenes set here: Throughout (perimeter scenes, deaths, exploration)

## Motifs & Symbols

**The Count / The Math**
- First introduction: Ch 1 — Cole counts forty-seven faces in the signing room
- Subsequent appearances: Every chapter. The running headcount is the novel's pulse. Cole's internal count tracks from 47 to 24.
- Thematic function: Freedom reduced to arithmetic. The dehumanizing logic of survival vs. the humanizing weight of each number becoming a face. The central tension of the book.

**The Humming Trees**
- First introduction: Ch 3 — first night on the planet
- Subsequent appearances: Ch 9 (hum deepens with second cycle), Ch 27 (final scene)
- Thematic function: The planet's indifference and beauty. Neither welcoming nor hostile — just present. What the colonists learn to live alongside rather than conquer. Cole describes them in his first message to Lily.

**Elena Vasik's Logs**
- First introduction: Ch 6
- Subsequent appearances: Ch 8, 10, 11, 14, 17, 23
- Thematic function: Ghost narrative. The story that almost was Cole's. The mirror that shows what happens when a colony stops being a group. Structural device: nested story-within-story, read in fragments.

**Clause 9.4.2**
- First introduction: Ch 1
- Subsequent appearances: Ch 5, Ch 9, Ch 15, Ch 24, Ch 25, Ch 27
- Thematic function: The contract as both trap and key. Corporate language weaponized against its authors. The bureaucratic fine print that accidentally enabled freedom.

**Messages to Lily**
- First introduction: Ch 9 — three sentences about the trees
- Subsequent appearances: Ch 19 (the truth), Ch 26 (increasing frequency), Ch 27 (final message)
- Thematic function: Cole's interior emotional arc externalized. Each message marks a stage of transformation. The first is avoidance. The second is honesty. The last is invitation. Fatherhood as practice, not possession.

**The Creep**
- First introduction: Ch 11
- Subsequent appearances: Ch 12, 13, 18
- Thematic function: The planet's cyclical biology. Destruction that is also renewal. The threat that becomes solvable only when the colonists stop fighting the planet and start understanding it. Mirrors the colony's own need to adapt rather than impose.

**The Ancient Channels**
- First introduction: Ch 12
- Subsequent appearances: Ch 13, 18
- Thematic function: Something lived here before and built infrastructure for the cycles. The colonists aren't pioneers — they're inheritors. Connects to theme of learning to live within a system rather than extracting from it.

**The Cairn**
- First introduction: Ch 23 — Cole builds it at the ghost colony site
- Subsequent appearances: Once only. Does not need repetition.
- Thematic function: Cole honoring Elena Vasik. Acknowledging the dead without sentimentalizing them. Stones, not words.
