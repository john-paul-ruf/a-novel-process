CLIENT VOICE PROFILE
====================
Sentence Rhythm:       Varied and instinct-driven. Medium sentences dominate but
                       interrupted by short declarative punches for emphasis:
                       "They were all bad. Some just less bad than others." /
                       "Freedom." Uses fragments as interior thought. Piles
                       clauses with commas in longer passages, building raw
                       momentum rather than polished cadence. Parallel
                       construction for rhetorical force when the moment calls
                       for it.

Vocabulary Register:   Colloquial-smart. Never pretentious but never dumb.
                       Comfortable dropping into casual register ("god forsaken,"
                       "what the hell," "pretty cute," "bazillion years") while
                       embedding intellectual observations naturally — a 16-year-old
                       character who draws parallels to the Pilgrims without it
                       feeling forced. Technical terms used casually, not
                       ceremonially. Strong analogy instinct carried over from
                       nonfiction voice — reaches for historical and practical
                       parallels to ground abstract stakes.

Dialogue Style:        Sparse and naturalistic. Characters say only what's
                       necessary. Subtext carried through action beats and small
                       physical details (a sad smile, an imperceptible pause, a
                       flipped name tag). Dialogue advances plot and reveals
                       character simultaneously. "Said" and "asked" preferred;
                       action beats over tags when possible.

Emotional Temperature: Dark warmth. Cynical surface over an earnest core.
                       Characters are guarded but observant — they notice beauty
                       and tenderness even in hostile environments (the cute
                       anarchist, Trish's smile, dreaming of trees). Sentiment
                       is never announced — it leaks through what the character
                       chooses to look at. Hope is stubborn and unspoken.

Interiority Depth:     Very deep — the default mode. Most narrative lives inside
                       the character's head. Thoughts rendered as direct internal
                       monologue without italics. The character's reasoning
                       process shown in real-time (calculating odds, weighing
                       risks, reading contracts). This is the writer's comfort
                       zone and primary narrative engine.

Punctuation Habits:    Periods for emphasis over em-dashes. Fragments used
                       freely in interior thought. Semicolons rare in fiction
                       (more common in nonfiction). Commas occasionally run long
                       in compound sentences. Clean and functional — punctuation
                       serves pace, not decoration.

Structural Instincts:  Linear chronological with natural intercuts of reflection
                       and backstory woven into present action. Alternates
                       between scene (dialogue, action) and summary/reflection
                       fluidly. Comfortable embedding exposition inside character
                       interiority (reading a contract becomes world-building).
                       Drawn to third-person limited with deep POV. Influenced
                       by nested/layered narrative structures (King's The Wind
                       Through the Keyhole, Jordan's Wheel of Time multi-POV
                       approach).

Tonal Anchors:         Pragmatic dreamer — calculates the odds, then signs the
                       contract anyway. Dystopian cynicism with stubborn hope
                       underneath. Irreverent humor: dry, observational,
                       self-aware ("Who else would be stupid and desperate enough
                       to take such risks"). The nonfiction's scientist-mystic
                       becomes the fiction's clear-eyed survivor who still
                       bothers to dream.

Avoid:                 Purple prose; sentimentality that hasn't been earned;
                       adverb stacking; passive construction; overly polished
                       or "writerly" sentences that break the raw, direct voice;
                       humor used as constant deflection (irreverence yes,
                       clowning no); said-bookisms beyond "said" and "asked";
                       exposition dumps that aren't filtered through character
                       consciousness; polysyndeton (chaining clauses or items
                       with repeated "and" — the "and and and" technique) —
                       never use; recursive "and the X was the Y" constructions
                       (e.g., "and the silence was the answer," "and the waiting
                       was the worst part") — limit to rare, high-impact moments
                       at most; "the not-X was the X" inversions (e.g., "the
                       not-knowing was the knowing") — never use.

## Notes on Voice-to-Character Alignment

The writer's nonfiction and fiction voices diverge significantly. Nonfiction is
measured, warm, instructional — the teacher-guide. Fiction unlocks a darker,
more irreverent, more interior mode — the pragmatic dreamer trapped in systems
bigger than himself. The fiction voice is the one to inhabit for prose work.

Key calibration points:
- Interiority is the primary narrative engine. When in doubt, go deeper inside
  the character's head, not wider into description.
- Humor is irreverent and observational, never performative. It emerges from
  how the character sees the world, not from authorial cleverness.
- Hope is always present but never stated outright. It lives in what the
  character notices (trees, a girl's smile, the word "freedom").
- World-building is delivered through character consciousness — what they
  read, what they calculate, what they resent, what they dream about.
- The writer naturally embeds intellectual depth (historical parallels,
  systemic critique) without breaking character voice.
